# RP-001 acceptance criteria

These criteria define the first technically valid, human-reviewed,
disqualify-only rule pack. They do not authorize activation or a material
recommendation.

## Package-level acceptance

- [ ] At least one material/service pair was deliberately selected by the
  verified-human creator from the two canonical gap axes.
- [ ] No material/service pairing was generated by an agent, LLM, matrix
  migration, fuzzy matcher, or punctuation heuristic.
- [ ] Every selected service/media concept resolves to one exact, currently
  approved MED-NORM media ID.
- [ ] Every selected material identity and scope is explicitly supported; a
  family has not been represented as an approved compound or component.
- [ ] Every rule is `unvertraeglich` or opaque `bedingt`; the pack contains no
  `vertraeglich` rule or positive statement.
- [ ] Every rule is atomic over exactly one material, one canonical media ID,
  and one non-empty condition.
- [ ] Every rule's complete statement equals exactly one approved primary
  claim.
- [ ] Every rule and reference is covered; no extra or hidden rule exists.

## Evidence acceptance

- [ ] The exact MAT-GOV-03A snapshot, domain pack, content hash, and rule refs
  are pinned.
- [ ] Every source has document ID, revision, edition, content digest, and a
  derived `source_ref`.
- [ ] Every source is used; every claim has at least one source; every claim is
  bound to one rule under MAT-EVID-01A.v1.
- [ ] Every claim has exact atomic scope and a derived `claim_ref`.
- [ ] Every claim text is at most 512 Unicode characters and 1024 UTF-8 bytes.
- [ ] Sources, claims, bindings, and set-like arrays are duplicate-free and in
  canonical order.
- [ ] No URL is the sole source identity and no licensed full text is stored in
  the aggregate or repository.

## Factual-review acceptance

- [ ] MAT-EVID-01C covers every and only every 01A source and claim.
- [ ] All source identities and claim scopes match 01A exactly.
- [ ] Source metadata, locators, rights, and excerpts meet all character/byte
  bounds.
- [ ] Every rights state is `permitted`, `licensed`, or `public_domain`.
- [ ] Every claim contains all reviewer-declared required source types.
- [ ] Primary claim type is exactly:
  - `incompatibility` for `unvertraeglich`; or
  - `conditional_compatibility` for opaque `bedingt`.
- [ ] Supporting types are limited to `temperature_limit`,
  `application_limit`, and `regulatory_constraint`.
- [ ] `other_technical` media-identity claims are reviewed separately and do
  not enter the MATERIAL-RULES claim set.
- [ ] Final conflict/supersession relations are empty.
- [ ] Creator, reviewer, and owner-approver are pairwise different authenticated
  verified-human subjects with their exact required roles.
- [ ] Projection is exactly `reviewed / approved` with an intact append-only
  event hash chain.

## MED-NORM acceptance

- [ ] Catalog is tenant-bound, immutable, content-addressed, and loaded from
  PostgreSQL through repository authority.
- [ ] Media ID is derived from exact canonical name and identity kind.
- [ ] Aliases are exact, complete for the asserted identity, unique, sorted,
  and exclude canonical name.
- [ ] The exact identity assertion is supported by currently approved
  `other_technical` evidence.
- [ ] Each media-identity claim uses the typed Evidence Manifest v2
  `media_identity` scope with no `materials` field and exactly one `media_ref`,
  as ratified by `RP001-OD-01`.
- [ ] No material placeholder was invented and no v1 snapshot was converted or
  reinterpreted.
- [ ] No LLM output or free-text heuristic creates a canonical classification.
- [ ] Catalog authority remains `NORMALIZATION_ONLY`; positive statement is
  false.

## Runtime-seam acceptance

- [ ] MAT-EVID-01B is `bound_unreviewed` and pins exact 03A/01A IDs, hashes,
  versions, tenant, domain pack, and runtime identities.
- [ ] Missing, duplicate, foreign, reused, retargeted, or scope-drifted
  bindings fail closed.
- [ ] MATERIAL-RULES capability is repository-issued, non-serializable, and
  reloads/revalidates review, binding, ruleset, evidence, tenant, and catalog.
- [ ] Revocation, quarantine, retargeting, integrity drift, or catalog drift
  invalidates the held capability before use.
- [ ] Capability authority is exactly
  `FACTUAL_REVIEWED_DISQUALIFY_ONLY` and positive statements remain false.

## Required negative proofs

Each must be rejected independently:

- `vertraeglich` verdict;
- raw/free-text medium instead of a reviewed `med_*` ID;
- missing or second material, medium, or condition;
- rule statement not equal to exactly one primary claim;
- primary claim type/verdict mismatch;
- `other_technical` as a rule claim;
- unknown/restricted rights;
- missing required source type;
- non-empty conflict/supersession relation;
- creator self-review or creator/reviewer self-approval;
- cross-tenant access;
- source, claim, snapshot, catalog, or binding hash drift;
- duplicate, dangling, orphan, foreign, or non-canonical reference;
- automatic matrix or LLM-derived fact.

## Explicitly outside acceptance

Even after every checkbox passes, the following remain forbidden until later
owner decisions and independent gates:

- production migration;
- MAT-GOV-03C active pointer or deployment cohort;
- material sampling above zero;
- request-pipeline, API, prompt, serializer, cache, or frontend integration;
- public or positive material statement;
- application deployment;
- final engineering, manufacturer, legal, or conformity release.
